Product contains active ingredients at concentrations exceeding AICIS industrial chemical thresholds. At the assessed concentrations, the product meets the definition of a therapeutic good under the Therapeutic Goods Act 1989 and requires TGA registration prior to supply in Australia. Labelling and packaging
Launch blocker
Non-compliant
Current label does not meet TGA labelling requirements for listed therapeutic goods under TGO 92. Required elements absent include: AUST L number (obtainable only post-registration), advisory statements for specified ingredients, dosage instructions in the mandatory format, and required warning statements. The existing AICIS-compliant label cannot be used for TGA-regulated supply and must be fully revised following successful ARTG listing. Therapeutic Goods Order No. 92 (TGO 92); TGA Listed Medicines Evidence Guidelines 2021
Safety testing and certification
Requires attention
Existing safety data and certificates of analysis are adequate for AICIS purposes. However, TGA listed medicine requirements mandate additional evidence of product quality and safety. Microbiological testing to TGO 100 standards and heavy metals testing to Ph. Eur. standards will be required as part of the ARTG listing dossier. Current testing documentation should be reviewed and supplemented before submission. No testing needs to be repeated where existing data meets the standard. Therapeutic Goods Order No. 100 (TGO 100); TGA Evidence Guidelines: Microbiological Standards 2019

Requires attention
Technical file was compiled for AICIS registration. Substantial additional documentation is required for a TGA ARTG listed medicine application: complete formulation details, manufacturing site TGA GMP clearance confirmation, product specifications, stability data (real-time and/or accelerated), and evidence that each listed ingredient is included in the ARTG Ingredients Database at the applicable dose range. TGA sponsor designation must also be confirmed and documented prior to application. TGA Listed Medicines Evidence Guidelines 2021; TGA GMP Clearance Requirements for Overseas Manufacturers

Market notification and registration
Launch blocker
Non-compliant
No ARTG listing exists for this product in Australia. Supply of a therapeutic good in Australia without ARTG listing (or other applicable approval) is an offence under the Therapeutic Goods Act 1989. What happens next
Prioritised action plan — 5 steps to market
Prioritised action plan
1
Confirm TGA listed medicine pathway and appoint a TGA sponsor
Before any other regulatory steps, confirm that the ARTG listed medicine pathway is appropriate for the product and formally appoint an Australian-registered TGA sponsor. The sponsor will be the responsible legal entity for the ARTG application and ongoing regulatory obligations. This must be completed before any other registration steps are initiated. Without an appointed sponsor, no application can be submitted.
Launch blocker Regulatory reference: TGA Sponsor Obligations
2
Confirm overseas manufacturer's TGA GMP clearance status
Verify whether the manufacturing site already holds current TGA GMP clearance. If GMP clearance is not in place, initiate the clearance application as early as possible — this process runs in parallel to the ARTG listing application but must be resolved before listing approval. Delay in GMP clearance is the most common cause of extended ARTG application timelines.
Launch blocker Regulatory reference: TGA GMP Clearance — Overseas Manufacturers
3
Compile ARTG listed medicine application dossier
Prepare the full application dossier for submission via the TGA Business Portal. Required components include: product details and formulation, ARTG Ingredients Database confirmation at specified doses, stability data, microbiological and heavy metals testing to TGO 100 standards, manufacturing details, and sponsor declaration. Assess gaps in existing documentation against TGA evidence guidelines and commission additional testing where required.
Launch blocker Regulatory reference: TGA Listed Medicine Evidence Guidelines 2021
4
Revise product label to meet TGA TGO 92 requirements
Develop a TGA-compliant label in parallel with the ARTG application. Required elements include: AUST L number placeholder (updated upon approval), advisory and warning statements for specified ingredients, dosage instructions in the mandatory TGA format, sponsor name and address, and all required declaration statements. Label revision should be completed prior to ARTG listing approval to enable immediate post-approval supply.
Launch blocker Regulatory reference: TGO 92; TGA Labelling Guidance for Listed Medicines
5
Submit ARTG listed medicine application via TGA Business Portal
Submit the complete ARTG listed medicine application once the dossier is finalised and GMP clearance is confirmed (or in active process). Monitor the application status through the TGA Business Portal. Standard processing time is 3–6 months from a complete submission. Plan supply chain and inventory accordingly. Do not supply, distribute, or advertise the product in Australia until the AUST L number is issued and the product is listed on the ARTG.
Launch blocker Regulatory reference: TGA Business Portal — ARTG Application
