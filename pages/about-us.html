Our Story

How TecEx Group's Trade Expertise Became TGC's Compliance Foundation

TecEx Group was founded in 2001 with a clear purpose: to remove the complexity from international trade. For over two decades, the business built its reputation as a global leader in customs clearance, Importer of Record (IOR) and Exporter of Record (EOR) services, and Delivered Duty Paid (DDP) solutions - managing the logistics and trade compliance challenges that prevent technology hardware, industrial equipment, and commercial goods from moving freely across borders.

Over those two decades, a pattern emerged in the client engagements TecEx handled. Companies could clear customs successfully and still find their products blocked from sale - because a label was non-compliant, a substance exceeded a regulatory limit, a certification was missing, or a registration had not been completed in time. The trade compliance problem had been solved. The product compliance problem had not.

TecEx Global Compliance was founded to address exactly that gap. TGC brings the same depth of specialist expertise to product-level regulatory compliance that TecEx Group built in trade compliance - combining the group's international infrastructure, market knowledge, and operational reach with a dedicated team of product compliance specialists. Where TecEx Group clears products through borders, TGC ensures those products are built, certified, labelled, and documented to meet the requirements of every market they enter.

Today, TGC operates as TecEx Group's dedicated global product compliance division, covering seven core practice areas: chemicals, cosmetics, electronics, food and beverages, food supplements, consumer goods, and medical devices. We serve manufacturers, importers, exporters, and brand owners targeting the EU, UK, US, China, and other regulated markets worldwide.

How We Work

The Principles Behind Every TGC Compliance Programme

Compliance consulting varies enormously in quality - from generic guidance that restates public regulation text to specialist advice grounded in the specific technical and commercial context of a client's product and markets. These are the principles that define how TGC works.

01

Regulatory Depth, Not Surface Coverage

Every TGC position is built on the actual text of regulations, official guidance, and verified primary sources - not generic compliance overviews or paraphrased third-party summaries. We read the legislation. We track the amendments. We understand how enforcement authorities interpret requirements in practice, not just how the texts read on paper. When we advise a client, the position is documented and defensible.

02

Multi-Market by Design

The EU, UK, US, and China each operate independent regulatory frameworks that interact in complex ways - sometimes aligning, often diverging, occasionally in direct conflict. TGC designs compliance programmes that address all target markets simultaneously, identifying where shared documentation and testing can satisfy multiple regimes and where market-specific responses are unavoidable. We do not manage four separate compliance workstreams and present them as a programme.

03

Commercial Context Always

Compliance advice is only valuable when it connects to a business decision. We deliver recommendations in the context of launch timelines, supply chain constraints, risk tolerance, and commercial priorities - not as abstract regulatory positions that leave the client to work out what to do next. Every TGC engagement concludes with a clear action plan: what needs to change, in which order, and by when, expressed in terms the business can act on.

04

Speed Without Shortcuts

Backed by TecEx Group's operational infrastructure and market presence across 200+ countries, TGC delivers compliance programmes at a pace that supports commercial timelines - without compromising on accuracy. When an expedited audit is needed, we can mobilise the right specialists within days. When a market entry deadline is fixed, we build the compliance programme around it. Speed is a capability, not a reason to cut corners.
