Confirmed HS code
8518.30 Headphones and earphones, whether or not combined with a microphone, and sets consisting of a microphone and one or more loudspeakers.

International 6-digit HS code (WCO Harmonized System, 2022 edition)

WCO HS 2022 — Chapter 85, Heading 8518
TARIC code (EU)
8518.30.95 00 Other — wireless headphones and earphones, not combined with a microphone, for use with mobile telephones and other wireless devices.

This is the correct 10-digit TARIC subheading for wireless Bluetooth headphones with no integrated microphone, imported into any EU member state. The product does not fall within the more specific subheadings for hearing aids (8518.30.10) or for telephony headsets with integral microphone (8518.30.80). Classification at 8518.30.95 is confirmed.

Written rationale

The product is a wireless audio device designed to reproduce sound received via Bluetooth. Classification proceeds as follows under the General Rules of Interpretation (GRI):

GRI 1 Classification is first determined by the heading that most specifically describes the product. Heading 8518 (microphones, loudspeakers, headphones, earphones) specifically covers headphones and earphones per the Explanatory Note to 8518, which includes wireless devices operating via Bluetooth or other radio transmission.
GRI 6 Classification at subheading level is determined by applying GRI 1–5 mutatis mutandis. Subheading 8518.30 covers headphones and earphones, whether or not combined with a microphone. The product does not combine a microphone and is not a hearing aid, placing it correctly within 8518.30.95 (other).

Note 3 to Chapter 85 confirms that apparatus for the transmission or reception of voice, images, or other data, including apparatus for communication in a wired or wireless network, falls within Chapter 85. The product is not excluded by any chapter note. No alternative heading provides a more specific description.

ECCN screening
US Export Control Classification: EAR99

The product has been assessed against the Commerce Control List (CCL) under the US Export Administration Regulations (EAR). Consumer wireless audio devices operating via Bluetooth are not captured by any specific ECCN on the CCL. The product is therefore classified as EAR99 — items subject to EAR but not specifically listed on the CCL. No US export licence is required for shipment to most destinations under EAR99.

EU dual-use screening: Product does not appear in Annex I of EU Dual-Use Regulation 2021/821. No EU export authorisation required.
Standard controls apply: EAR99 classification does not exempt shipments from OFAC sanctions screening, SDN list checks, or end-user/end-use controls. These apply to all exports regardless of ECCN.
Deemed export: No deemed export licence required for this product. EAR99 items do not trigger deemed export rules.

Risk flags
No import licence or permit required. Wireless headphones classified under 8518.30.95 are not subject to import licensing in the EU. The product is not on any restricted or prohibited goods list.
Radio Equipment Directive (RED) — 2014/53/EU: The product is a radio device (Bluetooth) and is subject to the RED for EU market access. CE marking, technical documentation (Technical File), Declaration of Conformity, and ETSI standard compliance testing are required. This is a product compliance requirement, not a customs restriction, but it must be resolved before the product can be placed on the EU market.
RoHS Directive — 2011/65/EU: Consumer electronics are subject to RoHS restrictions on hazardous substances. Confirmation of RoHS compliance is required as part of the CE marking process. If a Substance of Very High Concern (SVHC) in the product is present above 0.1% w/w, REACH SVHC notification obligations also apply.
Packaging and labelling: Country of origin marking is not mandatory under EU customs law for this product category, but is recommended. Language requirements for labelling apply at the member state level. The OECD Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) requirements for electronics packaging may apply depending on the destination member state.
RED Directive 2014/53/EU; RoHS Directive 2011/65/EU; REACH Regulation (EC) 1907/2006
BTI advisory
BTI not recommended for this product

A Binding Tariff Information ruling from the relevant EU member state customs authority is not recommended at this time for the following reasons:

The classification at 8518.30.95 00 is unambiguous. The product description matches the TARIC subheading without material borderline risk.
The MFN duty rate is 0%, meaning the financial benefit of a BTI locking in this classification is negligible. A BTI would primarily be of value where a lower-duty classification is in dispute.
A BTI would be advisable if: (a) the product description changes materially, (b) the product is borderline between 8518.30 and another heading, or (c) a high-volume programme creates commercial value in legally binding the classification for 3 years.
EU Customs Code (Regulation 952/2013), Articles 33–37; EU BTI application guidance (TAXUD)
