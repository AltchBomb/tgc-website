Critical Cosmetic Compliance Dates: 2025–2027 Enforcement Timeline

The enforcement dates that should be driving reformulation, documentation updates, and label changes for any brand selling cosmetics in the EU or UK. Missing these deadlines means products cannot be placed on the market.

This article is part of our cosmetics compliance coverage.

← View our Cosmetic Compliance services page

These are the enforcement dates that should be driving reformulation, documentation updates, and label changes for any brand selling cosmetics in the EU or UK. Missing these deadlines means products cannot be placed on the market — and products already on shelf must be withdrawn by the sell-through date.

1 Sept 2025

EU — Omnibus VII in force

22 CMR substances added to Annex II (banned). TPO — used in gel nail products — banned with no transition period. Source: Regulation (EU) 2025/877.

1 May 2026

EU — Omnibus VIII applies (Regulation EU 2026/78)

Perborate derivatives and further CMR substances banned. Modifications to Annexes II, III, IV, and V. Source: Regulation (EU) 2026/78, published 13 January 2026.

15 Jul 2026

UK — 4-MBC UV filter ban; formaldehyde labelling threshold tightened

4-MBC banned from being placed on the GB market. Formaldehyde-releaser warning threshold drops from 0.05% to 0.001%. Source: UK SI 2026 No. 23.

31 Jul 2026

EU — New nano ingredient prohibitions apply

Nano ingredient prohibitions under Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/858 apply for products placed on market from this date.

15 Aug 2026

UK — 16 CMR substances banned from market placement

Includes TPO, clothianidin, dimethyl propylphosphonate, dibutyltin maleate. Sell-through ends 15 February 2027. Source: UK SI 2026 No. 23.

1 Jan 2027

EU — Proposed: 15 further CMR substances banned (WTO notified May 2025)

Includes Triphenyl Phosphate. Sell-through until July 1, 2028. Subject to final adoption in 2026. Source: WTO notification G/TBT/N/EU/1140.

4,137 Record Safety Gate alerts in 2024 — highest since the system launched in 2003, with cosmetics the most flagged category Source: European Commission Annual Report, April 2025
36% Share of all 2024 EU Safety Gate alerts accounted for by cosmetics — the most flagged product category for the second consecutive year Source: European Commission, 2025
9,528 Active US facility registrations under MoCRA as of January 2025 — nearly double the total ever registered voluntarily under the prior VCRP programme Source: US FDA, March 2025
